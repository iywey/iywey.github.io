Bio

Hi, I’m Wey Xie.

I did my Bachelor’s in Business Administration at the Frankfurt School of Finance & Management and worked at Deutsche Bank. I realised this wasn’t for me and booked a oneway ticket to Ko Samui—I wanted to become a high stakes poker player because I believed the journey would require me to expand my understanding of the world and lead to the personal growth I was yearning for.

Fast forward two years, I became one of the best. I travelled the world and had fun. But, I missed meaning. Parents prepare their kids for failure but not for immediate success. I was living the life of my dreams but felt unsatisfied as it did not bring the sense of completion I expected.

This led me to a two year long spiritual oddysey, where I lived alone on mountaintops and with Buddhist monks in the middle of lush forests. I read philosophy and religion. I meditated and experimented with psychedelics. I sought enlightenment. This journey revealed many facets of life; it was a true fairy tale I‘m blessed to have experienced. But, it ended with me losing all my money, my relationship, and my mind.

I returned back to Germany and went through desperate times. I felt like a complete failure, and my parents reinforced that by reminding me how I threw away my promising career for gambling and drugs. I was depressed and wanted to take my life. As I was planning that, one thought crossed my mind. I was curious to see what would happen if I gave life everything I had, one final time. I imagined what the best version of myself looked like and committed to a relentless pursuit of my highest ideal. I needed that, for that's the only thing that gave me a slimmer of a reason to live.

I grinded hard and reintegrated into society. I got accepted into the leading computer science university in Germany. My goal was to work on BCIs (Brain-Computer Interfaces) and AGI (Artificial General Intelligence).

Yet, it was a painful contrast, being a high stakes poker player, the world my oyster, to now being back in university, broke, surrounded by 18-year-olds, living in a small dorm room with a roommate I disliked. And nobody cared about my past. I was nobody.

But deep inside I knew who I was. After my first semester I decided I needed to do something about my situation and take care of myself first before changing the world. I started playing poker again and founded a poker and coaching business. I also founded a software company.

Then, I had a spinal cord injury. For over six months, I was locked to the floor of my dorm room. Again, I found myself in a situation asking me to give up. But, having made it through hell once, I knew I could do it again. I used my time to build out my businesses and educate myself by reading lots of books. The situation worsened when I made the decision to get operated on my spine, as the operation went bad and I suffered nerve damage. If I experienced hell before, then this third visit was hell^10. Through a lot of hard work and perseverance, things gradually got better.

My businesses were successful, and I spent the last two years accumulating wealth and taking care of my health and the structure of my life. I’m in a good position now. My life isn’t perfect, but my adventure so far has allowed me to gain wisdom that I’ll carry with me for the rest of it. I enjoy a lot of freedom in how I choose to spend my time, and it‘s something I reflect on deeply. Over the last years, I‘ve tried out many things and outgrew many existential crises. I‘ve learned a lot. It‘s been a luxury to live this life.

Finally, I decided to return to my original mission. I want to work on BCIs—my goal being to enhance our cognition and bring us closer to digital immortality. It‘s a big word and a big dream, but I‘d rather dream big and fail than not having tried. So I’m going back to university. I might get a PhD.

Still, I'll continue building businesses to create wealth, invest to grow it, and read and write to further expand my understanding of this mesmerising world we live in. I‘ll continue doing lots of sports because sports are the best. And while many might know me as a disciplined, serious individual, I can be silly and goofy around the people who I love and who love me. I‘ll continue seeking out and deepening the relationships that make life worth living.

Enjoy my essays, and if you resonate, reach out.

Wey
