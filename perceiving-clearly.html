Perceiving Clearly

April 2023

When I wanted to learn how to think better, all the advice I found was about making better decisions. But that’s only one part of thinking clearly. What about perception—the way we interpret events?

Perception is biased: our conscious minds don’t just display raw sensory data; it gets interpreted first. What you experience in this moment is a filter of reality. We know we can make better decisions, but we’ve never been taught that we can better filter. Yet, that’s more important.

Think about it this way: if you would perceive things differently, then your actions would change. Thus, if your perception is clouded—which it is—then you’re often acting in ways that are vastly different than how you would act if you were seeing what’s true.

As you can imagine, we don’t fix this by snapping fingers. We're creatures of habit, and we’ve been conditioned to perceiving this way since birth.

Here’s one question to help you start shift out of it:

In this moment, how would your best version perceive life?
