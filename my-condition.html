My Condition

July 2023

I’m a biological, mortal animal. My consciousness is an emergent property of physical phenomena, and it will cease when I die. Therefore, I have limited time to experience what it means to be alive, and it’s worth contemplating deeply how I want to spend this time.

Though, in another way, I don’t really exist. My sense of self too is an emergent property of physical phenomena, and the idea of a continuous self is an illusion. The “I” that existed in the past is not the “I” that is experiencing now, and the “I” of the future will be someone (or something) else again, even on a cellular level. In seven years not a single cell of your body will be the same. Anxieties and concerns for the future are illusions. There is no reason to be afraid of death because I won’t be there to experience it.

I’m a mortal animal that desires to exist and to exist well; simultaneously, I don’t exist at all. My life is a dance within this paradox.
